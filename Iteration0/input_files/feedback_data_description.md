The observed failure of the Dual Kalman Filter (DKF) to maintain parameter estimation accuracy in the latter stages of the observation period is directly attributable to the dataset's physical model and constraints. The dataset description specifies that the oscillators are underdamped, meaning the displacement $x(t)$ decays exponentially over time ($A * \exp(-\gamma * t)$). As the signal amplitude approaches zero while the Gaussian measurement noise remains constant, the effective Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) decreases over the 20-second duration. This temporal degradation of the signal quality explains why the DKF loses track of the damping coefficient $b$ as the oscillator approaches equilibrium, as noted in Section 4 of the results.
