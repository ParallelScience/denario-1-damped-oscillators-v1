**Title**: Defining the Observability Threshold for Damping Identification in Stochastic Underdamped Systems

**Description**: This study characterizes the fundamental limits of parameter recovery by quantifying the Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) threshold required to accurately estimate the damping coefficient ($b$) and spring constant ($k$) using noisy $x(t)$ and $v(t)$ observations. We will compare the performance of a discrete-time Kalman Filter—designed to handle state-space noise—against a central-difference numerical derivative approach to determine the minimum temporal resolution and SNR required to maintain an estimation error below 5% of the ground-truth values. By systematically downsampling the 500 time steps and injecting synthetic variance into the displacement and velocity fields, we will map the phase space of reliable parameter extraction. The final output provides a rigorous diagnostic of the dataset's physical fidelity, establishing the statistical boundary where Gaussian noise renders the inherent damping characteristics unrecoverable. This framework defines the operational limit for state-space observers in underdamped systems where acceleration data is absent.
